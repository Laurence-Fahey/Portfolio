Sound Engineering Diploma - Pro Tools Mix & Master

For the final assessment in my sound engineering diploma at London AMP, I mixed and mastered a song in Pro Tools. I chose this track featuring Celina Ann from a LEWITT mixing contest. You can listen to the mixed and mastered versions below. I put a lot of care into using automation to get the levels of the tracks right and the EQ balance of the instruments. I'm particularly happy with the tone of the double-bass and that it cuts through the mix appropriately. For the master I chose Spotify as my destination platform, setting the loudest section to -14 LUFS. I used a reference track and applied some minimal EQ corrections, light compression, tasty saturation, and reverb.

Audio Repair - Irish Folk Songs

Here is an example of some audio repair I have done using Izotope's RX8 software. A family friend found some recordings from the 60's of my Grandad, uncles, and friends singing Irish folk songs. They were recorded on a tape recorder in a kitchen and had some unsurprising quality issues such as clipping, background noise and hiss, and a low-end hum throughout. Using the RX8 software, I was able to eliminate or (tastefully) reduce these issues. The result is a much more intimate and enjoyable listening experience. You can listen to the original and repaired versions in the Soundcloud player below.

Luthiery

Martin 000 Steel String Acoustic Guitar

I've wanted to build a guitar since I was a teenager. When I saw London Metropolitan University offered an affordable guitar building course, I couldn't resist. It was a traditional luthiery course, preferring sharp hand tools over machinery, so no cheating and everything was created from scratch. The teacher, Nick, is a brilliantly enthusiastic luthier and taught us as much about the physics of guitar building as the woodworking. I chose to build a Martin 000 style guitar for its suitability to fingerstyle playing. I planed the neck from mahognany, the top from cedar, and the back and sides from stripey African khaya maghogany. The next steps will be to bend the sides and 'box-up' the body, followed by carving the fingerboard and bridge. I might cry a little if it sounds good when I'm finished... or if it doesn't!

Rosette
Rosette channel routed. Purfling bent, glued, and planed flush with the cedar top.
Bracing
Bracing being glued to the cedar top in a 6mm radius dish.
Coming Together
Neck shaped, top and back radiused, and bracing glued. Sides ready for bending next.
